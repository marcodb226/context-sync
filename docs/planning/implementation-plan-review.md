## Reviewer Notes

- The draft is in good shape on candidate-source traceability, future-work defer/drop decisions, and milestone readability. The keep/defer/drop table in section 1.2 is thorough and each decision links back to its source artifact.
- I did not find a need to reopen the overall milestone shape. The foundation-first sequencing is well-motivated and the four-milestone arc tracks naturally from primitives through whole-snapshot behavior to incremental flows and finally CLI packaging. The main issues are dependency gaps, prerequisite clarity, and validation strategy completeness rather than a fundamentally wrong release sequence.
- The dependency DAG already permits useful parallelism that the milestone grouping does not highlight: [M3-3](implementation-plan.md#m3-3---diff-mode-and-lock-aware-drift-reporting) (diff) depends only on M2-2 and M2-3, not on [M3-1](implementation-plan.md#m3-1---incremental-refresh-and-quarantined-root-recovery) or [M3-2](implementation-plan.md#m3-2---add-and-remove-root-whole-snapshot-flows). Once Milestone 2 is complete, diff work can proceed in parallel with refresh and root-mutation work rather than waiting for them. This is not a plan defect, but it is a scheduling optimization worth noting for the owner response.
- The risk register correctly front-loads the `OQ-1` freshness validation as the highest-impact risk. PLAN-R4 above calls out a specific instance of the `linear-client` coverage risk (the batch freshness query) that deserves explicit attention alongside the general domain-layer audit recommended in PLAN-R2.
- The plan's treatment of the ADR review outcomes is consistent: ADR-R2a (lock metadata), ADR-R3 (tiered traversal), and ADR-R4 (root quarantine) are all reflected in named ticket scope, while the deferred and discarded items are correctly excluded.
